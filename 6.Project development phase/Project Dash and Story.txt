PROJECT DASHBOARD & STORY — HEART DISEASE ANALYSIS

1. Introduction
The Tableau Dashboard provides a complete visual analysis of the Heart Disease dataset containing 4500+ individuals.  
The goal of this dashboard is to visually identify which factors contribute most to heart disease and to help users or healthcare professionals understand patterns easily.

The dashboard contains multiple charts such as:
- Age vs Heart Disease
- Race vs Heart Disease
- Gender vs Heart Disease
- BMI vs Heart Disease
- Stroke, Physical Activity, Sleep Time, Skin Cancer, Asthma impact
- General Health Influence

-----------------------------------------------------------
2. Dashboard Components
-----------------------------------------------------------

A) Age vs Heart Disease  
- This horizontal bar chart shows that heart disease risk increases with age.  
- People aged **55–59, 60–64, and above** show the highest number of cases.  
- Younger age groups (20–29) have very low heart disease counts.

B) Race vs Heart Disease  
- Pie chart shows the distribution of heart disease across racial groups.  
- Majority belong to **White population (70.47%)** followed by **Black (24.18%)**, and others.

C) Heart Disease Based on Age Category  
- A second age-based visualization showing frequency distribution.  
- Helps confirm that older age groups contribute more to heart disease cases.

D) Stroke vs Heart Disease  
- Bar chart clearly shows that people who had a past **Stroke** are at a much higher heart disease risk.  
- Stroke is a major contributing factor.

E) Sex vs Heart Disease  
- Bar chart reveals that **Males have higher percentage risk (36.44%)** compared to **Females (52.82% no risk)**.  
- Gender plays a significant role in risk prediction.

F) BMI vs Heart Disease  
- Pie chart shows average BMI of individuals.  
- Higher BMI is strongly correlated with heart disease.  
- Obesity is a clear risk factor.

G) Skin Cancer vs Heart Disease  
- Visualization shows how those with skin cancer have higher heart disease counts.  
- Indicates other chronic illnesses also increase risk.

H) Asthma vs Heart Disease  
- Bar chart indicates that asthma patients show elevated risk levels.  
- Chronic breathing issues affect heart performance.

I) General Health Impact  
- Scatter/bubble chart clearly shows that as general health decreases (Fair, Poor), heart disease counts increase.  
- “Poor” health group shows maximum risk.

J) Physical Activity vs Heart Disease  
- Chart shows that individuals who **do not engage in physical activity** have higher heart disease rates.  
- Exercise plays a crucial preventive role.

-----------------------------------------------------------
3. Story (Sequential Tableau Story Flow)
-----------------------------------------------------------

Slide 1 — Overview of Dataset  
- Introduces the distribution of individuals with and without heart disease.

Slide 2 — Lifestyle Risk Factors  
- Smoking, Alcohol Drinking, SleepTime, and Physical Activity patterns.
- Lack of sleep or unhealthy habits increase risk.

Slide 3 — Medical Risk Factors  
- Diabetes, Stroke, Asthma, Skin Cancer contribute heavily to disease.

Slide 4 — Demographic Analysis  
- Age and Gender are strong indicators for higher disease probability.

Slide 5 — General Health Improves Prediction  
- Poor general health correlates strongly with heart disease.

Slide 6 — Final Insights  
- Summary of key factors
- Preventive suggestions like improving physical activity and lifestyle.

-----------------------------------------------------------
4. Insights from Dashboard
-----------------------------------------------------------

✔ Older adults (55+) are the highest risk group  
✔ Stroke & Diabetes drastically increase risk  
✔ Males are more likely to develop heart disease  
✔ Poor general health = higher heart disease probability  
✔ High BMI is a major contributor  
✔ Lack of physical activity increases risk  
✔ Asthma and skin cancer patients show elevated risk levels  
✔ White & Black racial groups hold majority of cases
